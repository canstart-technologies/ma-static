Optimized Nesting for Material Savings

Smart Part Placement

Our CAM software analyzes your part geometries and automatically arranges them on the sheet for maximum material utilization, minimizing waste and reducing your material costs significantly.

Common Line Cutting

Where possible, parts are nested to share common edges, reducing punching time and material waste. This technique is especially effective for rectangular panels and brackets.

Cost Transparency

We provide detailed nesting layouts with material utilization percentages so you can see exactly how efficiently your parts are produced and where cost savings are achieved.

Remnant Management

Usable remnant sheets from your production are tracked and can be utilized for future smaller parts, further reducing material waste and costs.

Frequently Asked Questions

What's the minimum hole size you can punch?

Minimum hole diameter is approximately equal to the material thickness (e.g., Ø1.5 mm for 1.5 mm thick material). Smaller holes require laser cutting or drilling.

Can you punch stainless steel?

Yes, we regularly punch SS304 and SS316 up to 3 mm thick. Stainless requires more force and specialized tooling, which we have in our Vasai facility.

How does nesting reduce my costs?

Efficient nesting reduces material waste by 20–30%. You're charged only for the material used, plus a small scrap allowance. Better nesting = lower material cost per part.

What file formats do you accept?

We accept DXF, DWG, STEP, and SolidWorks files. 2D flat patterns in DXF format are ideal for fastest turnaround.

Can you combine punching with bending and welding?

Absolutely! Our integrated Vasai facility offers punching, laser cutting, bending, welding, and powder coating — complete turnkey fabrication under one roof.
