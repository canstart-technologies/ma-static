Overview

Our welding and fabrication services transform sheet metal components into complete, structurally sound assemblies. Whether it's a single prototype cabinet or a batch of welded frames, we deliver clean, strong welds with minimal distortion. Our skilled welders work with MS, SS, and aluminium to produce enclosures, boxes, and custom fabricated parts that meet your exact specifications.

Welding Processes

MIG Welding

Fast, efficient welding for MS and aluminium. Ideal for high-volume production of cabinets and structural frames with consistent quality.

TIG Welding

Precision welding for stainless steel and thin materials. Clean, aesthetically pleasing welds for visible surfaces and food-grade applications.

Spot Welding

Resistance spot welding for joining overlapping sheet metal. Fast cycle times for brackets, panels, and enclosure assemblies.

Frequently Asked Questions

Which welding process is best for my application?

MIG is ideal for MS and aluminium with fast production speeds. TIG is preferred for stainless steel and thin materials where aesthetics matter. Spot welding works best for joining overlapping sheets quickly. We'll recommend the best process during DFM review.

Can you weld parts that were cut or formed elsewhere?

Yes, we can weld customer-supplied components. We'll inspect incoming parts for fit-up and quality before welding to ensure strong joints.

Do you offer finishing after welding?

Absolutely. We provide weld grinding, surface preparation, powder coating, and hardware installation. Fully finished assemblies ready for use.

What tolerances can you hold on welded assemblies?

We typically hold ±1 mm on overall dimensions for welded assemblies. Tighter tolerances possible with jigs and fixtures — discuss your requirements with us.
